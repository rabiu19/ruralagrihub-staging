Development of Bambara Groundnut Value Chain based on Regenerative Agricultural Principles in Northern Ghana

Bambara Groundnut value chain development based on regenerative agriculture principles project is a long term project being facilitated by the Pond Foundation (Not for Profit Organization based in Switzerland) in partnership with WhatIF Foods (a Singaporean company), and AgriHub company Ltd (A Ghanaian company in Tamale, Ghana).
Together, we seek to build a regenerative Bambara Groundnut (BGN) supply chain anchored in smallholder farmersâ fields in the Northern Region around Tamale.

The project seek to support smallholder farmers to increase their yields while improving their soils and community environment. The ultimate goal is to improve farm family livelihoods and overall well-being which will come from improved farming practices, increased yields and prices, sensitive cultural engagement, improved services and an enhanced community environment.

The project officially commenced on February 3rd 2022 with The Pond Foundationâs Scott Poyntonâs arrival in Accra. Poynton is now stationed for the next few months in Tamale and is building a team with close partnership with Rural AgriHub Ltd and planning the work ahead. This means building a local team to engage with farmers while bringing in practical resources such as seed and technical advice around how to grow the BGN in the most productive way. It means facilitating the commercial business too, making sure that good quality BGNs are exported, at the right price, to WhatIF Foodsâ partner, Synthite, in India.

In preparation for the growing season that begins in June/July 2022, the Pond Foundation team lead, Scott Poynton with team from Rural AgriHub set out a series of Actions to invite farmers to join and engage with the project.
The 2022 plan identified 5 communities in each of the 5 Districts in Northern Region (total 25 communities) to partner with us in the production of Bambara Groundnuts (BGNs). Our approach is to support the farmers with seed, technical information and general advice and guidance to ensure good yields and at least 500 MT of purchased BGNs in early 2023. As the project goes forward, our intention is to grow the level of support to the farmers to introduce new regenerative farming techniques that can support more yield increases and soil improvement. For 2022, the focus is on getting into the communities, getting known, understanding the baseline socio-economic and environmental context, securing production and yield increases and establishing trust-based relationships that will serve as the foundation for a long engagement.

In each community, we will identify 2 Community Facilitator (CF) from amongst the community who will act as our contact person and who will be the conduit for our outreach to the farmers and for us to receive questions and feedback from the farmers. This is the model through which we will work with the smallholder farmers. We will work with the CFs to control and manage seed distribution. We will train them in improved production techniques using the CRI/SARI Production Manual that will be developed as part of the project, and will support them to understand and implement our approach to data collection. The CFs will be our voice, eyes and ears on the ground to support the farmers and monitor production. As the project expands, we will work through the CFs to introduce regenerative agriculture techniques and to explore other interventions, for example, agroforestry, biodiversity projects etc.

As we journey with farmers within this exciting project, join us to learn more about it by taking a story on our social media handles or website every week.

About the Company
Rural AgriHub is a social enterprise integrating sellers from rural areas with buyers of fresh fruits, vegetables and general agricultural products in urban areas. We deliver a wide range of both organic and non-organic products directly from farms to stores of B2B customer retailers, wholesalers, food processors, restaurants, exporters and institutional kitchens.
